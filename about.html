I'm interested in programming, where do I start?
Well, that's a loaded question that depends on a lot of factors, and I'm probably not the most qualified advisor for this info. That being said, I can tell you what I did to start, and what I would have done differently were I to do it again. The single most useful piece of advice I can give you is to PRACTICE WHAT YOU ARE THE WORST AT! Seriously, I spent the first 6 months of my programming career using a singular class in Java, only because that's what I was comfortable with. It resulted in complicated headaches of projects, and a complete lack of understanding of class relations and dependencies. Practice the things you're not comfortable with(which also applies to many other things in life). Other than that, I would suggest focusing on Pseudocoding (writing code down without worry of syntax/compile errors) firstly, then get used to the actual programming language and worry about syntax. Have an objective, start small, and stop at nothing to achieve said objective.
